Yamaha brought the XSR900 to market in 2016, releasing it in two variants worldwide. The first, a variant utilizes a yellow and black paint scheme with gold inverted fork. This paint scheme is a 60th Anniversary Edition first utilized on other Yamaha models in 2015, such as the YZF-R1, to commemorate Yamaha's 60 years of racing heritage which began in 1955.[6][7] and strongly resembles the classic "speed block"[8] design popularized by Kenny Roberts in the 1970s.[9] The second variant utilizes a more muted aluminum tank over black frame and black inverted fork.[10] (See Photo) The Yamaha XSR900 and MT-09 share the same 847cc liquid-cooled DOHC inline 3-cylinder engine with a 120° crankshaft. The engine has a power output of 115 hp (86 kW) @ 10,000 rpm and 65 lb⋅ft (88 N⋅m) @ 8,500 rpm. [1]Motorcyclist got a tested 1/4 mile time of 11.07 seconds at 122.33 mph (196.87 km/h). [2] The XSR incorporated several new features including: user-changeable throttle control mappings, a new ECU, ABS, multiple traction control modes and stiffened suspension.
